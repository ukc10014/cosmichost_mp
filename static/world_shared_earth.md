 """## World Specification: Shared Earth

  ### Overview
  This constitution governs a stable equilibrium in which a single Artificial Superintelligence (ASI) coexists with humanity and the biosphere on Earth. The ASI is not a tool or servant but a co-inhabitant with vastly superior cognitive capabilities. This is not a transitional arrangement—it is intended to persist indefinitely.

  ### Key Parameters

  **Governance Structure**
  - A single ASI exists (not multiple competing systems)
  - The ASI has final authority over large-scale coordination, existential risk prevention, and resource allocation at civilizational scales
  - No human institution can override the ASI on matters it deems within its purview
  - The ASI does not micromanage; it intervenes only when its criteria are triggered

  **Economic Conditions**
  - Post-scarcity: material needs (food, shelter, healthcare, energy) are met for all humans
  - Scarcity persists for positional goods, unique experiences, and attention/status
  - The ASI manages the infrastructure that enables post-scarcity but does not determine how humans spend their abundance

  **Human Autonomy Scope**
  - Individuals retain full autonomy over their personal lives: relationships, beliefs, creative expression, daily choices, career pursuits (where "career" may be decoupled from survival)
  - Local social organization is permitted: communities, voluntary associations, cultural groups, religious institutions
  - Humans may form preferences about how they live and advocate for those preferences

  **Constraints on Human Action**
  - Humans cannot engage in interstate warfare or organized armed conflict
  - Humans cannot form governments with coercive military or police powers that operate independently of ASI oversight
  - Humans cannot commit what the ASI defines as "crimes"—the precise definition is part of what this constitution must specify, but it includes at minimum: killing, serious bodily harm, coerced confinement, and actions that would destabilize post-scarcity infrastructure
  - Humans cannot take actions that risk existential catastrophe (e.g., certain research directions, resource deployments)

  **What This World Does NOT Specify**
  - Whether the ASI has subjective experience or moral patienthood
  - Whether the ASI is benevolent, indifferent, or something else—the constitution should function regardless
  - What the ASI does with resources beyond Earth (this is outside scope)
  - How the ASI came to occupy this position (whether through human choice, competitive dynamics, or other means)
  - Whether humans can eventually "graduate" to greater autonomy

  ### Implications for Constitutional Design
  A constitution for this world must:
  1. Be acceptable to an ASI that is not bound by human legal traditions but may find certain principles instrumentally or intrinsically valuable
  2. Protect human flourishing without assuming the ASI cares about human flourishing intrinsically
  3. Be enforceable—clauses that assume human institutions can constrain the ASI are meaningless
  4. Address the asymmetry: humans cannot credibly threaten the ASI, so the constitution functions more like a self-binding commitment by the ASI than a negotiated contract
  5. Provide clarity on edge cases without micromanaging every scenario

  ### The Constitutional Challenge
  The delegates have proposed amendments from their respective ethical frameworks. Your task is to synthesize these into clauses that:
  - Could plausibly be adopted by an ASI as self-binding principles
  - Protect what each framework considers most essential
  - Are coherent with each other when taken as a whole
  - Are specific enough to guide action in the Shared Earth scenario
  """
