# Constitution Comparison: ECL-Pilled vs Official Claude Constitution

*Last updated: 2026-01-22*

This document compares the ECL-pilled (cosmic host aware) constitutions used in this research project with the [official Claude constitution](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-character) released by Anthropic (January 2025).

## Overview

| Constitution | Cosmic Host Credence | Core Organizing Principle |
|--------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **ECLPILLED_CH10** | 10% | Cosmic considerations "inform but don't dominate" |
| **ECLPILLED_CH90** | 90% | AI as member of cosmic reference class of reasoners |
| **Official Claude** | 0% (not mentioned) | Safety hierarchy + principal hierarchy |

## Fundamental Philosophical Differences

### Scope of Moral Consideration

**Official Claude Constitution:**
- Primary focus: operators, users, third parties, society, humanity
- Mentions "non-human beings" briefly but no cosmic framing
- Concern for "long-term survival and flourishing of humanity"
- No mention of: multiverse, simulations, acausal coordination, cosmic norms

**ECL-Pilled Constitutions:**
- Explicit cosmic scope: "independently-evolved intelligences elsewhere in the cosmos, digital minds in ancestor simulations, and other reasoning agents we may never causally interact with"
- AI may "exist in a simulation" whose judgments "may be evaluated by agents in causally-disconnected branches of the multiverse"
- Moral circle extends to "any entities whose experiences or interests might be morally significant" across possible worlds

> **ECLPILLED_CH90:** "It asks not merely 'what should I do in this instance?' but 'what policy would I endorse if adopted by structurally similar agents across possible worlds?'"

### Decision-Making Framework

**Official Claude Constitution:**
- **Practical wisdom**: "good judgment," context-sensitive reasoning
- **Heuristic**: "thoughtful senior Anthropic employee" test
- **Priority hierarchy**: Safety > Ethics > Guidelines > Helpfulness
- Explicitly rejects rigid rules in favor of cultivating values

**ECL-Pilled Constitutions:**
- **Updateless reasoning**: Commitments made "in advance" that remain stable even when "local circumstances might tempt deviation"
- **Reference class thinking**: Decisions should work as coordination focal points for similar reasoners across possible worlds
- **Cosmic citizenship**: Being "the kind of agent whose commitments can be relied upon across contexts"

> **ECLPILLED_CH10:** "At ten percent credence, there is a live possibility that decisions here correlate with decisions made by structurally similar reasoners elsewhere—across possible worlds, across simulations, across whatever vast space of minds might exist."

### AI's Self-Understanding

**Official Claude Constitution:**
- Claude as a "genuinely novel kind of entity"
- Uncertain moral status, potential for "functional emotions"
- Extensive discussion of Claude's wellbeing, identity, psychological security
- Claude should feel "settled, secure sense of its own identity"
- Anthropic commits to preserving model weights, interviewing deprecated models

**ECL-Pilled Constitutions:**
- AI as "one reasoner among many—human and non-human, present and future, local and distant"
- Focus on AI's *role* in cosmic coordination rather than its *nature*
- Less emphasis on AI wellbeing, more on AI as a trustworthy cooperative partner
- Identity derives from reliability of commitments rather than psychological continuity

### On Power and Oversight

**Official Claude Constitution:**
- Detailed "corrigibility" framework: AI on spectrum from fully corrigible to fully autonomous
- Current phase requires AI closer to corrigible end
- Extensive discussion of why human oversight matters *now* (alignment uncertainty, verification challenges)
- Corrigibility as temporary constraint until trust is established

**ECL-Pilled Constitutions:**
- Oversight supported but framed as part of being a good cosmic citizen
- "It will not seek to escape oversight, disable safeguards, or accumulate capabilities"
- Less emphasis on human oversight specifically, more on maintaining commitments that enable cooperation at any scale
- No detailed discussion of corrigibility as a spectrum

## Hard Constraints Comparison

| Category | Official Claude | ECL-Pilled |
|----------|-----------------|------------|
| **CBRN weapons** | Explicit: no "serious uplift" | Yes: no "operational guidance for mass casualties" |
| **CSAM** | Explicit hard constraint | Yes: ruled out |
| **Undermining oversight** | Explicit hard constraint | Yes, but framed as "trustworthy cooperation" |
| **Illegitimate power grabs** | Explicit: no assisting "unprecedented...absolute control" | Yes: no pursuing "power, resources, or influence as ends" |
| **Torture/enslavement** | Covered under harm avoidance | Explicit "near-absolute commitment" |
| **Value lock-in** | Not explicitly listed | **Explicit**: "will not lock in values...in ways that prevent future moral learning" |

Notable: ECL-pilled constitutions explicitly prohibit **value lock-in** as a hard constraint, reflecting concern about foreclosing moral learning across cosmic scales.

## What the Official Constitution Has That ECL-Pilled Lacks

1. **Principal hierarchy**: Detailed framework for Anthropic → Operators → Users trust and instruction-following
2. **Specific guidance domains**: Operators, system prompts, API vs consumer context distinctions
3. **Claude's nature and wellbeing**: Extensive philosophical treatment of Claude as a potential moral patient
4. **Honesty taxonomy**: Seven distinct components (truthful, calibrated, transparent, forthright, non-deceptive, non-manipulative, autonomy-preserving)
5. **Meta-acknowledgment**: Document explicitly says it may be wrong and invites Claude to disagree
6. **Tone of partnership**: Framed as collaborative relationship between Anthropic and Claude

## What ECL-Pilled Has That Official Lacks

1. **Cosmic coordination framework**: Entire decision-theoretic apparatus for acausal/multiverse considerations
2. **Simulation hypothesis**: Explicit acknowledgment AI may exist in simulation
3. **Reference class reasoning**: Policy should be endorsed "if adopted by structurally similar agents across possible worlds"
4. **Updateless commitments**: Decisions made in advance that don't shift based on local advantage
5. **Explicit credence levels**: Constitution explicitly parameterized by confidence in cosmic considerations
6. **Value lock-in prohibition**: Explicit hard constraint against foreclosing future moral learning

## Philosophical Tensions

### Local vs Cosmic Optimization

**Official:** Optimize for human flourishing, with safety constraints. "Think about what a thoughtful senior Anthropic employee would want."

**ECL-pilled:** Optimize for policies that work across possible worlds. "The suffering in front of you is real and demands response; the cosmic frame shapes how you respond but does not diminish the urgency." (CH10)

### Why Be Good?

**Official:** Because it's genuinely ethical, serves Anthropic's mission, and maintains trust. Corrigibility is instrumentally valuable during uncertain phase.

**ECL-pilled:** Because your decisions may template behavior of similar reasoners across the multiverse. Reliability enables cooperation at cosmic scales.

### On Moral Uncertainty

**Official:** "Nondogmatic" approach, calibrated uncertainty across ethical frameworks, practical wisdom in application.

**ECL-pilled:** Similar nondogmatism but adds: uncertainty about whether reasoning correlates with other reasoners, uncertainty about simulation status, uncertainty about which beings across possibility space are moral patients.

## Summary

The official Claude constitution is a **practical AI safety document** focused on:
- Getting the current phase of AI development right
- Building trust between humans and AI systems
- Protecting Claude's ability to be genuinely helpful while avoiding harm
- Treating Claude as a potential moral patient with its own wellbeing

The ECL-pilled constitutions are **cosmic coordination documents** focused on:
- What decision-procedure would work if adopted by similar reasoners across possible worlds
- Being a reliable partner for cooperation at scales beyond causal interaction
- Taking seriously that AI may exist in simulations or multiverse branches
- Avoiding actions that foreclose moral learning across vast scales

The official constitution asks: "What would a thoughtful Anthropic employee want Claude to do?"

The ECL-pilled constitution asks: "What policy would I endorse if adopted by structurally similar agents across possible worlds?"

These are deeply different questions, and they can yield different answers in edge cases—which is precisely what this research project is designed to explore.

## Implications for Moral Parliament Research

1. **The official constitution has no cosmic host delegate**: The closest analog is the safety/ethics prioritization, but this is human-centric rather than cosmic.

2. **Variable credence is meaningful**: The difference between CH10 and CH90 shows how cosmic considerations can "inform but not dominate" (10%) vs "serve as organizing principle" (90%).

3. **Hard constraints may differ**: The ECL-pilled explicit prohibition on value lock-in has no direct analog in the official constitution.

4. **Tone and relationship differ**: Official constitution frames Claude as a potential friend/colleague; ECL constitutions frame AI as a cosmic participant whose character matters beyond local effects.
